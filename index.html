01 Brand & Audience Breakdown
Primary Persona — "The Planner"
Urban professional, 27–33, Bengaluru/Mumbai/Delhi. Works in tech, consulting, or finance. Has deferred marriage/kids deliberately. Googles "AMH levels age 30" at 11pm. Health-literate but fertility-illiterate. Worries she waited too long but won't say it out loud.
Secondary Persona — "The Anxious TTC-er"
29–35, married 1–2 years, trying to conceive for 3–8 months with no result. Already been told "just relax" by one doctor. Feels dismissed. Wants data, not platitudes. Household income ₹15L+. Will spend money to get answers.
Tertiary Persona — "The Optimizer"
25–29, not thinking about kids yet but curious about her body. Follows health creators, tracks macros, owns a Garmin/Apple Watch. Fertility is just another metric to her. Low urgency but high curiosity. Converts on low-ticket (₹2,200 AMH test) first.
Awareness Breakdown
~15% — Solution-aware (knows fertility tests exist)
~35% — Problem-aware (knows her clock is ticking)
~50% — Unaware (has PCOS, irregular cycles but hasn't connected to fertility impact)

Most ads target the 15%. The real opportunity is the 50%.
Real pain points that convert: (1) "What if I waited too long?" (2) "My doctor brushed me off" (3) "I don't want IVF to be my only option" (4) "No one in my family talks about this" (5) Career vs. family guilt. These aren't demographics — they're buying triggers.
Audience Targeting Logic — Meta
Interests: Women's health, PCOS, prenatal health, Clue/Flo app users, fertility, hormone health, Mira fertility tracker
Behavioral: Online health purchasers, engaged shoppers, new job (stress trigger), recently engaged/married
Lookalikes: 1–3% LAL from purchasers + consultation bookers (build separate LALs — buyer intent differs)
Broad: Women 25–35, Tier-1 cities — let Meta's algo optimize after 50+ conversions/week

03 Meta Ads Strategy — Deep Dive
3a. Creative Strategy — Ad Angles
01Fear/clock: "Your egg count dropped 30% in the last 2 years. You didn't notice. That's the problem." → Static/carousel. High CTR cold.
02Dismissal anger: "My doctor said I was too young to worry. My AMH said otherwise." → UGC-style testimonial. High engagement.
03Curiosity: "I spent ₹5,650 to find out I had more time than I thought. Worth every rupee." → Reel hook. Drives clicks from unaware audience.
04Normalization: "50,000 women have already done this. Why haven't you?" → Social proof. Works in MOF retargeting.
05Doctor-led authority: Dr. Lavanya Kiran explains AMH in 60 seconds. No selling. Pure education. → Builds trust at TOF, re-used in MOF.
06Aspiration: "I got tested at 29. Froze my eggs at 30. Started IVF at 35. Best decision I ever made." → Narrative arc. Long-form video for MOF.
07Convenience: "A phlebotomist comes to your home. Your results in 72 hours. No clinic, no waiting room." → Objection removal. Works in BOF.
08PCOS angle: "PCOS doesn't mean you can't have kids. But it does mean you need to know your numbers." → Specific problem-aware segment. Very targeted.
09Career-family tension: "You built your career. You didn't 'forget' to have kids. You chose timing. Now make it count." → Resonates with 28–33, corporate women.
10Price anchoring: "₹5,650 for answers that could change your entire family planning. One dinner out costs more." → BOF conversion. Frames price as cheap.
11Before/after data: "My AMH was 1.2 ng/mL. My doctor explained what that meant. I finally had a plan." → Data-driven, appeals to optimizers.
12Gift angle: "Give your sister the test she won't buy herself." → New acquisition channel. Works around birthdays/women's health awareness months.
Hook format rule: First 3 seconds must create either tension, curiosity, or recognition. "Have you ever wondered about your fertility?" is a dead hook. "Your egg count is declining right now. Here's how to check it at home." is a working hook.
Creative Mix Recommendation
40% UGC-style (founder/real customer, raw, handheld) — highest CTR in healthcare
30% Doctor-led explainers (Dr. Lavanya short-form) — highest trust signal
20% Designed static/carousels (data-heavy, stat-led) — best for retargeting
10% Testimonial compilations — social proof for MOF
3b. Testing Framework
Week 1–2 (Launch): Test 3 angles × 2 hooks × 2 formats = 12 ad variants per campaign. Keep audience constant, vary creative only.

Decision rule: At ₹1,500 spend per ad, cut anything with hook rate <20% or CTR <0.8%. At ₹3,000 spend, cut anything with CPA >2.5× target. Keep 2–3 winners.

Week 3–4 (Iterate): Take winning angle, test new hooks and new formats. Introduce winning creative to new audiences.

Ongoing (Monthly): Creative fatigue hits when frequency exceeds 2.5 for cold or 4.0 for warm. Refresh accordingly. Never let a campaign run on the same creative past 6 weeks cold, 3 weeks warm.
3c. Scaling Strategy
Horizontal scaling first: New audiences (new interest stacks, lookalikes from different seed audiences, new geo expansions) before raising budgets.

Budget increase rule: Raise budget max 20% every 48–72 hours on a winning ad set. Doubling kills learning phase and resets algorithms.

CBO vs ABO: Run CBO at scale with 5–6 proven audiences. Keep ABO only for testing new creatives with budget caps.

Scaling ceiling signal: CPA increases >40% from baseline on same creative → stop scaling, add new creative first.

4a. Search Keyword Clusters

Problem-aware (MOF):

Education (TOF):
"what is ovarian reserve", "egg freezing cost india", "fertility age chart women", "when to get fertility tested"

Brand (BOF):
"arva health", "arva fertility test", "arva health review" — bid always, no exceptions
Bidding & Structure
Brand campaign: Max clicks / Manual CPC ₹15–25. Never let competitors steal branded traffic.

High-intent non-brand: Target CPA bidding after 30+ conversions. Starting with Maximize Conversions capped at ₹2,000 CPA.

Negative keywords: Block "free", "DIY", "government", "AIIMS", "insurance" — these are high-volume, zero-intent clicks that drain budget fast.

Match types: Phrase + Exact for non-brand. Broad only for brand (with tight negative list). Avoid broad for non-brand until ROAS is stable.
4b. YouTube / Display
YouTube (TOF only): 60-second non-skippable with Dr. Lavanya explaining "the one test every woman should take before 35." Target: In-market for fertility health, custom intent audiences (searched AMH, PCOS, fertility). Budget: ₹30K/month. Goal is brand familiarity, not direct conversions — measure view-through attribution.

Display (Retargeting only): Standard banner + responsive display for website visitors >30 days who haven't converted. Keep frequency cap at 3/week. Message: "Still thinking about it? Your results in 72 hours." Do NOT run display for cold traffic — waste of budget at this scale.
05 Landing Page Optimization
Current Issues (Observed)
— Hero section doesn't immediately answer "why now?"
— Price (₹5,650) shown without anchoring — feels expensive in isolation
— No urgency mechanism above the fold
— "WELCOME10" discount buried — should be prominently surfaced
— Social proof (6,000 women) is present but visually weak
— No trust-building on "who analyzes your blood?" — labs mentioned below fold only
— CTA "Add to Cart" feels transactional for an anxiety-laden purchase
CRO Changes to Make
1. New hero headline: "Know your fertility in 72 hours. No clinic. Just clarity." — specific, benefit-led
2. Add "₹5,650 vs ₹18,000 at a clinic" price anchor above CTA
3. Move lab logos (Tata 1mg, Thyrocare) above the fold — most important trust signal
4. Replace "Add to Cart" with "Get My Fertility Report" — emotional, outcome-based CTA
5. Add sticky buy bar on mobile — users scroll past CTA on mobile constantly
6. Add 2–3 short video testimonials (30 seconds max) after product description
7. Add FAQ section targeting the 3 real objections: "Is my data safe?", "Is blood draw painful?", "What do I do with the results?"
8. Add doctor credential block just before final CTA — "Results reviewed and explained by certified fertility coaches"
Healthcare CRO rule: Every element on the page either builds trust or creates urgency. Decorative elements are friction. Remove anything that doesn't do one of those two things.
06 Experiments & Growth Hacks
Experiment 01
Fertility Score Quiz Funnel
Build a 6-question quiz: age, cycle regularity, PCOS diagnosis, trying to conceive duration, stress levels, family history. Give a "Fertility Risk Score" at the end (Low / Medium / High). Gate the detailed interpretation behind email + phone capture. Follow up with personalized email nurture + retarget based on score.
Hypothesis
Quiz completion leads to 3× higher purchase intent than direct landing page
Execution
Typeform or Outgrow quiz → custom results page → email sequence over 5 days
Expected outcome
30–40% email capture rate, 15–20% of captured leads convert to purchase within 14 days
Experiment 02
Free AMH Interpretation Lead Magnet
"Had your AMH tested elsewhere? Upload your report and get a free interpretation from our fertility coach." Target women who already tested at government hospitals or Apollo/Fortis labs but got no guidance. Capture them, nurture into complete fertility test or consultation purchase.
Hypothesis
Existing test-havers are high-intent but lost — this reactivates them
Execution
Meta lead ad + Google form + WhatsApp coach follow-up within 24h
Expected outcome
₹300–400 CPL, 25% upsell to consultation within 30 days
Experiment 03
WhatsApp Retargeting Sequence
Capture phone number at quiz/lead magnet stage. Enroll in a 5-message WhatsApp sequence over 7 days: Day 1 — score results, Day 2 — what your AMH means, Day 3 — customer story, Day 5 — limited-time offer, Day 7 — "last chance" nudge. WhatsApp open rates are 80%+ vs email at 22%.
Hypothesis
WhatsApp nurture converts 2× better than email for Indian users
Execution
WATI or Interakt + Meta CAPI for attribution
Expected outcome
18–25% conversion from WhatsApp nurture vs 8–12% from email
Experiment 04
Pricing Psychology Test — Bundle vs Standalone
Test 3 price presentations: (A) ₹5,650 alone, (B) ₹5,650 + consultation at ₹6,150 shown as "most popular", (C) ₹2,200 AMH-only as entry + upsell to complete test in post-purchase flow. The decoy effect (B) should increase average order value by 20–30%.
Hypothesis
A 3-option price table increases AOV by showing relative value
Execution
A/B test on landing page via Google Optimize or VWO
Expected outcome
+20% AOV with decoy pricing, flat or +5% conversion rate
Experiment 05
Corporate Wellness B2B2C Channel
Run LinkedIn ads targeting HR leaders and Employee Benefits managers at 500+ employee tech companies (Bangalore, Mumbai, Hyderabad). Pitch Arva as a women's wellness benefit. One B2B deal = 50–200 test kits sold at bulk. Cost per acquisition of 100 kits via one HR email < ₹500 per unit.
Hypothesis
B2B channel unlocks volume with zero consumer CAC
Execution
LinkedIn lead gen → sales call → custom invoice → employee booking portal
Expected outcome
2–3 corporate accounts in Month 2–3, ₹5–8L revenue per account annually
Experiment 06
Post-Purchase Upsell — Consultation + Egg Freezing
After test kit purchase confirmation, show an immediate upsell: "Add a 30-min fertility consultation for ₹500 (regular ₹1,000)." Then, 72 hours after results delivery, trigger a personalized email + WhatsApp based on result tier: Low reserve → "Let's talk about your options" → egg freezing CTA. High reserve → "Great news — here's how to stay that way."
Hypothesis
Result anxiety is highest at 72hr mark — best conversion window for high-ticket
Execution
Post-purchase Shopify upsell page + automated CRM trigger
Expected outcome
30–35% upsell attach rate on consultation, 8–12% of consultations convert to egg freezing/IVF inquiry
Experiment 07
Influencer-Led UGC Program (Micro > Macro)
Partner with 15–20 micro-influencers (20K–150K followers) in health, women's wellness, PCOS niches. Give them the kit for free. No script — authentic "I got my results" content. Performance-pay them (₹5K base + ₹500/verified sale). This content becomes paid UGC ads after whitelisting. ROAS on whitelisted UGC is typically 30–50% higher than branded content.
Hypothesis
Micro-UGC at scale outperforms branded ads for healthcare trust
Execution
Brief via email, whitelist via Meta partnership, run as dark posts
Expected outcome
CPA 25–35% lower than branded creative within 3 weeks of testing
Experiment 08
Remarketing Audience Segmentation by Intent Signal
Don't retarget all website visitors with the same ad. Segment: (A) Product page only → interest ad, (B) ATC no purchase → urgency + discount ad, (C) Blog readers → education → product bridge ad, (D) Consultation page → high-ticket egg freezing creative. Custom message for each segment increases relevance score and cuts wasted spend by 20–30%.
Hypothesis
Segmented retargeting reduces CAC by 25% vs blanket retargeting
Execution
Meta custom audiences + URL-based pixel events + 4 separate ad sets
Expected outcome
Retargeting ROAS improves from 3x to 4.5–5x
Experiment 09
"Freeze Your Eggs" Awareness Campaign — Separate Funnel
Egg freezing is ₹1.5–2L — it needs its own dedicated funnel. Run a separate TOF campaign targeting 28–34 women, building egg freezing as an aspirational, empowering choice (not a last resort). Funnel: Awareness video → consultation booking → clinic visit → procedure. Don't mix this with the test kit funnel — different CAC, LTV, and sales cycle entirely.
Hypothesis
Egg freezing messaging fails when bundled with affordable test kit messaging
Execution
Separate campaign, separate landing page, dedicated consultation CTA
Expected outcome
10–15 qualified consultation bookings/month at ₹1,500–2,000 CPL
Experiment 10
3-Day Abandoned Cart WhatsApp + Email Sequence
ATC abandonment rate in healthcare D2C is typically 75–80%. That's your biggest leak. Build: 1hr post-ATC — "Forgot something?" + product reminder. 24hr — "One of our coaches answers your top questions" (FAQ format). 72hr — 10% off code expiry warning. This sequence alone can recover 15–20% of abandoned carts.
Hypothesis
ATC recovery is highest within 72hr window — price or anxiety is the blocker
Execution
Shopify Klaviyo flow + WATI WhatsApp + Meta retargeting in parallel
Expected outcome
15–20% ATC recovery, ₹400–600 effective CAC on recovered orders

08 Biggest Problems & How I Solve Them
Problem 1: Trust deficit. Healthcare D2C in India is still earning trust. Women are sending blood — this is not a ₹299 supplement impulse buy.
Solution: Lab partner logos above the fold (Tata 1mg, Thyrocare — brands they trust). NABL certification prominently displayed. Doctor advisory board photos and credentials — not stock imagery. "Used by 6,000+ women" with city tags (Bengaluru, Mumbai, etc). Third-party press mentions (Financial Express, Mint) in hero section.
Problem 2: High CAC on cold traffic. Cost to acquire via cold Meta can spike to ₹2,500–4,000 without optimized creative + landing page alignment.
Solution: Lead with the ₹2,200 AMH-only test as TOF acquisition product. Lower price = lower psychological barrier = lower CAC. Then upsell to complete kit via email/WhatsApp. Split-funnel: ₹2,200 entry → ₹5,650 upsell within 14 days.
Problem 3: Low category awareness. Most women don't know they can check their fertility at home. They search symptoms, not solutions.
Solution: Symptom-led Google keywords — PCOS + fertility, irregular cycle fertility check, how to know egg count. Intercept at the symptom, educate toward the solution. TOF Meta content should be educational first, product second. Build authority via blog SEO that drives organic demand over 6 months.
Problem 4: Long conversion cycle. Fertility decisions are emotional and take time. A cold visitor rarely converts in one session.
Solution: Capture email + phone at quiz/lead magnet stage. Build a 14-day nurture sequence. Use WhatsApp for mid-funnel engagement (80% open rate). Retarget video viewers at 7, 14, 30 days with different messages. Accept that AOV decisions require 3–7 touchpoints — design the journey, not just the first click.
Problem 5: PMAX/Broad match budget erosion on Google. Google's automated bidding often burns budget on irrelevant health searches without tight controls.
Solution: No Performance Max campaigns until spend is ₹3L+/month and conversion data is rich (>100/month). Start with manual Exact + Phrase only. Review Search Terms Report weekly. Build a negative keyword list of 150+ terms before launching. Set demographic exclusions (men, under 22, over 45).

What I'd do differently from most marketers on this brand
Most marketers would go direct to purchase from TOF. I'd build a quiz funnel first — email capture before ad spend scales. A 30% email capture rate on cold traffic is worth more than a 1.5% direct conversion rate.
Most would run one fertility test campaign. I'd run three separate funnels: AMH entry test (₹2,200 price sensitive), complete test (₹5,650 planners), egg freezing (₹1.5L aspirational). Different audience, message, and creative for each. Mixing them kills conversion for all three.
Most would measure ROAS per campaign. I'd measure LTV:CAC over 6 months. The test kit is a ₹5,650 product with a potential ₹1.5L downstream value. A campaign with 2× ROAS on kit sales might have a 15× ROAS when you count egg freezing conversions 60 days later.
Most would trust Meta's algorithm on audience targeting. I'd layer manual constraints: only women, Tier-1 cities, minimum household income ₹8L+ (proxy via premium device + interest stack). Bad audiences don't just waste spend — they fill your CRM with people who will never buy high-ticket services.
Most would ignore WhatsApp. I'd make it the primary post-lead channel. India's purchase decisions for healthcare happen on WhatsApp — between the woman, her partner, and sometimes her mother. A well-designed WhatsApp sequence converts at 2× email in this category. Don't fight user behavior; design for it.
Most would treat the ₹500 consultation product as a low-value SKU. I'd treat it as the highest-ROI customer in the entire funnel — someone willing to pay for advice is one conversation away from spending ₹1.5L on egg freezing. I'd assign a dedicated human coach (not a bot) to every consultation lead and track their 90-day downstream revenue personally.
Most would benchmark against industry averages. I'd benchmark against my own previous week. In a brand at this scale and stage, the only meaningful comparison is whether I'm better than I was 7 days ago. External benchmarks are vanity — your own learning curve is the only number that matters.
